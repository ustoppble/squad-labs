What it is

Not an editor. Not a black-box generator. An environment that orchestrates existing AI — Claude, Codex, Gemini and local models — as visible, steerable agents.

A grid, not a chat box

Each agent runs in its own isolated PTY pane inside a dynamic 2-column grid that grows from 1×1 to 2×3 as you add panes. You see every agent at once.

You orchestrate, AI executes

A conductor agent decomposes the work and delegates it to a squad. Overclock coordinates AI you already trust — it doesn't replace Claude Code, Codex or Gemini.

Event-driven by design

Workers wake the orchestrator the moment they hand off. No polling, no blocking — the conductor delegates, steps back, and resumes on its own.

How it works

The spawn → brief → handoff loop.

A real sequence, not a metaphor. This is the cycle the orchestrator runs for every unit of work.

01

Spawn

The orchestrator opens a visible worker pane bound to the active workspace, or invokes a worker on demand.

02

Brief

Once the pane has booted, the orchestrator sends a clear task to the worker — then ends its turn. No waiting.

03

Hand off

The worker does the job and calls handoff_submit. Its result is delivered straight to the orchestrator's input.

04

Synthesize

↻ loop

The orchestrator wakes, reads the handoff, and synthesizes. The worker auto-closes shortly after a clean handoff.

↻  The orchestrator never polls or blocks — it delegates, steps back, and is woken by the handoff itself.

Why it's different

A single assistant works for you. A squad works with you.

Single-agent assistant

  • One agent, working serially — one task at a time.
  • Subagents run invisibly; you can't watch or steer them.
  • One model for everything, whether it fits or not.
  • Spend is opaque until the bill arrives.

Overclock

  • Multiple agents run side by side in one grid — parallel by design.
  • Every agent is a visible pane you can read and steer — you're the pilot.
  • Mix Claude, Codex, Gemini and local models — the right one per function.
  • An orchestrator that can't cheat: a hard gate forces it to delegate, not do.
  • Live per-pane and aggregate squad cost meters — spend is always visible.
